# JTBD Job Stories — sbir-developer-feedback

## Job 1 — Capture Friction In-Context (Primary)

**Job Story**: When I hit a problem or suboptimal output while using the SBIR plugin, I want to capture my feedback immediately with the full context of what was happening, so I can report it without having to reconstruct or explain the state later.

**Functional dimension**: Quickly log a feedback entry (bug / suggestion / quality complaint) with one command.
**Emotional dimension**: Feel heard and confident the developer will understand what went wrong without a back-and-forth.
**Social dimension**: Be a good team contributor by flagging issues rather than silently working around them.

---

## Job 2 — Receive Actionable Feedback as Developer (Secondary)

**Job Story**: When a colleague submits feedback about the plugin, I want to receive a structured snapshot of what they were doing and what they experienced, so I can reproduce the issue and improve the plugin without interrogating them.

**Functional dimension**: Read a feedback file with enough context to reproduce or understand the issue.
**Emotional dimension**: Feel informed and able to act — not overwhelmed by vague complaints.
**Social dimension**: Demonstrate responsiveness to the team by acting on captured evidence.

---

## Job 3 — Rate Specific AI Output Quality

**Job Story**: When I review AI-generated content (images, past performance selections, section drafts), I want to rate specific aspects on a quick scale, so I can communicate quality signal without writing paragraphs of explanation.

**Functional dimension**: Score image quality, past performance relevance, writing quality, and topic scoring accuracy on 1–5 scales.
**Emotional dimension**: Feel like my quality bar is documented and communicable.
**Social dimension**: Help the developer see patterns across multiple proposal runs.
