# JTBD Job Stories -- Solution Shaper

## Persona: Phil Santos

**Who**: Solo SBIR proposal writer at a small defense tech company (10-50 employees). Technical founder who doubles as the proposal writer. Writes 3-5 proposals per year, 10-15 hours each. Pursues topics adjacent to core expertise as a growth strategy.

**Functional Job**: Systematically determine what technical approach to propose for a given solicitation before committing to weeks of proposal writing.

**Emotional Job**: Feel confident that the approach decision is grounded in evidence, not just the first idea that came to mind.

**Social Job**: Be able to articulate to teaming partners, TPOC, and evaluators exactly why this approach was chosen over alternatives.

---

## Job Stories

### JS-1: Deep-Read the Solicitation Beyond the Summary

**When** I have made a Go decision on a solicitation topic based on company-level fit scores,
**I want to** extract the full technical requirements, objectives, evaluation criteria, and constraints from the solicitation,
**so I can** understand what the solicitation actually needs at the level of detail required to generate meaningful candidate approaches.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Functional | Parse solicitation into structured requirements, objectives, evaluation criteria, and constraints |
| Emotional | Feel that I truly understand the problem, not just the title and abstract |
| Social | Be the proposer who reads deeper than competitors who skim the topic description |

**Traces to**: Opportunity O1 (Score 13)

---

### JS-2: Generate Non-Obvious Candidate Approaches

**When** I understand what the solicitation requires and I know my company's capabilities,
**I want to** systematically enumerate 3-5 technically distinct candidate approaches including non-obvious combinations,
**so I can** evaluate a real option space instead of defaulting to the first approach that comes to mind.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Functional | Generate multiple technically coherent candidate approaches from solicitation requirements and company capabilities |
| Emotional | Feel creative confidence that the option space has been properly explored |
| Social | Propose an approach that reviewers recognize as thoughtfully selected, not obvious |

**Traces to**: Opportunity O2 (Score 18)

---

### JS-3: Score Approaches Against My Company's Specific Strengths

**When** I have multiple candidate approaches for a solicitation,
**I want to** score each approach against my company's specific personnel expertise, past performance, IP, and TRL position,
**so I can** select the approach that maximally leverages what we are actually good at rather than what sounds impressive on paper.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Functional | Produce a scored comparison matrix across personnel alignment, past performance, TRL, solicitation fit, and commercialization |
| Emotional | Feel certainty that the selected approach plays to our strengths, reducing anxiety about competitive weakness |
| Social | Defend the approach selection to teaming partners and in the proposal narrative with evidence |

**Traces to**: Opportunity O3 (Score 19)

---

### JS-4: Evaluate Commercialization Potential Before Committing

**When** I am comparing candidate approaches and need to assess which has the strongest Phase III pathway,
**I want to** quickly evaluate each approach's dual-use potential, target transition programs, and estimated market relevance,
**so I can** select an approach that scores well on commercialization criteria and has a credible Phase III narrative.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Functional | Assess Phase III pathway strength, dual-use potential, and market relevance per candidate approach |
| Emotional | Feel confident the commercialization argument is real, not an afterthought bolted on during Wave 2 |
| Social | Present a commercialization section that evaluators (often technical, not business) find credible |

**Traces to**: Opportunity O4 (Score 14)

---

### JS-5: Converge on a Recommended Approach with Documented Rationale

**When** I have scored approaches and understand the tradeoffs,
**I want to** see a clear recommendation with rationale, runner-up analysis, and identified risks,
**so I can** commit to an approach with confidence and carry that rationale forward into the strategy brief and discrimination table.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Functional | Produce a structured recommendation naming the selected approach, scoring rationale, runner-up, risks, and discrimination angles |
| Emotional | Feel decisiveness -- the approach is chosen, the rationale is documented, the uncertainty is resolved |
| Social | Demonstrate to evaluators that the approach was systematically selected, not arbitrary |

**Traces to**: Opportunity O6 (Score 17)

---

### JS-6: Hand Off the Approach Decision to the Strategy Phase

**When** I have selected and approved a technical approach,
**I want to** produce a structured approach brief that the Wave 1 strategist can consume directly,
**so I can** proceed to strategy and research without re-explaining the approach decision or losing context between steps.

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Functional | Write an approach-brief.md artifact containing solicitation summary, selected approach, scoring matrix, runner-up, discrimination angles, risks, and Phase III quick assessment |
| Emotional | Feel relief that the hardest pre-proposal decision is made and documented |
| Social | Provide downstream agents (strategist, researcher) with clear direction rather than vague assumptions |

**Traces to**: Opportunity O8 (Score 11)

## Job Map (8-Step Universal) for Approach Selection

| Step | Goal | Current State | Desired Outcome |
|------|------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1. Define | Understand what the solicitation needs | Re-reads topic summary from Wave 0 | Minimize time to extract actionable requirements from solicitation |
| 2. Locate | Gather company capabilities and past work | Relies on memory of company strengths | Minimize effort to assemble company fit data per approach |
| 3. Prepare | Generate candidate approaches | First idea that comes to mind | Minimize likelihood of missing a strong candidate approach |
| 4. Confirm | Score and compare approaches | Gut feeling about which is best | Minimize uncertainty about which approach leverages company strengths |
| 5. Execute | Select and document the approach | Implicit decision, no record | Minimize time from scored approaches to documented selection |
| 6. Monitor | Validate commercialization potential | Deferred to Wave 2 (too late) | Minimize risk of selecting approach with weak Phase III pathway |
| 7. Modify | Adjust if new information arrives | Ad hoc during Wave 1-2 | Minimize effort to revise approach selection when picture changes |
| 8. Conclude | Hand off to Wave 1 strategist | Approach enters Wave 1 implicitly | Minimize information loss between approach selection and strategy |
